<p><p>The phosphate rock market is segmented into three main types: marine phosphate deposits, igneous phosphate deposits, and others. Marine phosphate deposits are formed from sedimentary processes and are often found in oceanic environments, providing a significant source of phosphate. Igneous phosphate deposits originate from volcanic activity and are characterized by high purity. The "others" category includes various lesser-known sources, such as altered volcanic rocks or bio-mineralized phosphates, contributing to diverse supply options in the phosphate market.</p></p>
