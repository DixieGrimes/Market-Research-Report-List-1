<p><p>The melt-spun fiber market is categorized into two main types: filament melt-spun fiber and staple melt-spun fiber. Filament melt-spun fiber consists of continuous strands, ideal for applications requiring strength and durability, such as textiles and industrial materials. In contrast, staple melt-spun fiber comprises shorter lengths, typically used in producing yarns and non-woven fabrics, providing bulk and softness. Both types serve distinct purposes across industries, including apparel, automotive, and home furnishings, driven by their unique properties and manufacturing processes.</p></p>
