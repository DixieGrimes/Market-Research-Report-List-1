<p><p>The electronic grade fiberglass yarn market is segmented based on thickness, influencing its applications in insulation and reinforcement materials. Thin yarns, between 5-7 microns, strike a balance between strength and flexibility, making them ideal for various electronic components. Ultrathin yarns, below 5 microns, provide superior insulation properties and are increasingly used in advanced electronics, including microelectronics and flexible circuits, due to their lightweight nature and enhanced performance characteristics.</p></p>
