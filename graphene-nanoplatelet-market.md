<p><p>The graphene nanoplatelet market is primarily segmented into hydrophobic and hydrophilic types. Hydrophobic graphene nanoplatelets are characterized by their water-repelling properties, making them suitable for applications requiring moisture resistance, such as coatings and composites. Conversely, hydrophilic graphene nanoplatelets interact favorably with water, enhancing compatibility in aqueous environments, and are ideal for applications in biomedical and environmental fields. The choice between these types depends on the specific requirements of end-use applications, influencing their demand and market dynamics.</p></p>
