<p><p>The wireless charging system market comprises two primary types of integrated circuits (ICs): transmitter ICs and receiver ICs. Transmitter ICs are responsible for generating and controlling the electromagnetic field to transmit power wirelessly to devices. These ICs manage power levels, communication protocols, and safety features. In contrast, receiver ICs convert the received electromagnetic energy back into electrical power to charge the device's battery. Together, these ICs enable efficient and effective wireless charging experiences across a range of consumer electronics.</p></p>
