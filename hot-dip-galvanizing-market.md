<p><p>The hot-dip galvanizing market comprises two primary types: batch type and continuous type. Batch-type galvanizing processes involve immersing a set quantity of steel parts in a molten zinc bath, making it suitable for smaller production runs or varied part sizes. In contrast, continuous-type galvanizing involves a continuous feed of steel strips through the zinc bath, allowing for high-volume production with consistent quality. Each type caters to different manufacturing needs, impacting efficiency, cost, and application suitability in various industries.</p></p>
