<p><p>The anti-peptic ulcer drugs market includes several key drug types: Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) reduce stomach acid production, effectively treating ulcers. Potassium-Competitive Acid Blockers (P-CAB) also inhibit acid secretion but through a different mechanism. Antacids neutralize existing stomach acid for quick relief. H2 Antagonists decrease acid production by blocking histamine receptors. Antibiotics are used to eradicate H. pylori infections linked to ulcers, while ulcer protective drugs provide a barrier to protect the stomach lining from acid damage.</p></p>
