<p><p>Urban transport systems encompass various market types, notably semi-rapid and rapid transport systems. Semi-rapid transport systems offer moderate-speed services, typically integrating buses and trams that efficiently connect urban areas. They focus on frequent stops and accessibility, catering to suburban commuters. In contrast, rapid transport systems, such as subways and high-speed trains, prioritize speed and efficiency, with fewer stops and higher capacity. These systems reduce travel time significantly, addressing the growing demand for efficient urban mobility in dense populations.</p></p>
