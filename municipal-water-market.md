<p><p>The municipal water market encompasses various treatment methods to ensure safe drinking water. Reverse osmosis membranes remove contaminants via pressure-driven filtration. Ion-exchange technologies remove dissolved ions, vital for water quality. Ultrafiltration (UF) membranes offer a barrier against bacteria and particulates. Electrodeionization (EDI) combines ion-exchange and electric current to produce ultra-pure water. Biological filtration uses microorganisms to break down pollutants. Chemical conditioning modifies water properties, while clarification separates solids. Filtration physically traps impurities, and chlorination disinfects water, ensuring safety.</p></p>
