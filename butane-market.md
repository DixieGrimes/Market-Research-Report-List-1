<p><p>The butane market consists of two primary types: N-Butane and Isobutane. N-Butane is a straight-chain hydrocarbon primarily used as a feedstock in petrochemical production, fuel blending, and liquefied petroleum gas (LPG). Isobutane, a branched isomer, is vital for producing isooctane for high-octane gasoline, as well as in refrigeration and as a propellant in aerosol products. Both types are derived from natural gas processing and crude oil refining, catering to diverse industrial needs and applications.</p></p>
