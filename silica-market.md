<p><p>The silica market is primarily categorized into three types: quartz, tridymite, and cristobalite. Quartz is the most abundant form, widely used in glassmaking and construction due to its durability. Tridymite and cristobalite, both high-temperature forms of silica, are less common but are essential in specialized applications like ceramics and refractory materials. These variations have distinct physical properties and crystal structures, influencing their usage across industries, including electronics, metallurgy, and food processing, thereby driving market demand.</p></p>
