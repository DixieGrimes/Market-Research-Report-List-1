<p><p>Plastic aspheric lenses are manufactured using various methods, each suited to different applications. Compression molding involves shaping heated plastic between two molds, ideal for high-volume production. Injection molding injects molten plastic into molds, allowing for intricate designs and efficient manufacturing. Turning molding uses a lathe to create precise shapes from plastic blanks, suitable for low-volume or specialized applications. Each method provides unique benefits in terms of cost, speed, and design flexibility, catering to diverse market needs in optics.</p></p>
