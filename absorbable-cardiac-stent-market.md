<p><p>Absorbable cardiac stents, also known as bioresorbable vascular scaffolds, are innovative medical devices designed to treat coronary artery disease. Unlike traditional metallic stents, which remain permanently in the body, these stents are made from biocompatible materials that gradually dissolve after serving their purpose of propelling the artery open. This unique feature reduces long-term complications, such as late stent thrombosis and restenosis, promoting natural vessel healing.</p><p>The Absorbable Cardiac Stent Market is witnessing steady growth, driven by advancements in biomaterials, increasing cases of coronary artery disease, and a growing preference for minimally invasive procedures. Clinical studies continue to support the efficacy of these stents, further encouraging their adoption. <p><ul><li>Polymer</li><li>Bomaterials</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The absorbable cardiac stent market is primarily divided into two types: polymer stents and bio-materials stents. Polymer stents utilize biodegradable polymers to provide temporary support to blood vessels, gradually dissolving over time, allowing natural healing. Bio-materials stents, on the other hand, use natural or synthetic materials that promote tissue integration and healing without permanent implantation. Both types aim to reduce long-term complications associated with traditional metal stents while facilitating improved patient outcomes in cardiovascular treatments.</p></p>
