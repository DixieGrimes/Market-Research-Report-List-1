<p><p>The anesthesia gas mask market comprises two primary types: disposable and non-disposable masks. Disposable masks are designed for single-use, enhancing infection control and minimizing cross-contamination risk, making them ideal for outpatient procedures and emergencies. Non-disposable masks, on the other hand, are reusable and constructed from durable materials, requiring proper sterilization after each use. They are often employed in longer or more complex surgeries. Both types cater to distinct needs in healthcare settings, ensuring patient safety and comfort during anesthesia administration.</p></p>
