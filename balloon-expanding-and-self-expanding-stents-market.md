<p><ul><li>Balloon Expanding Stents</li><li>Self-Expanding Stents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Balloon expanding stents are designed to be crimped onto a delivery balloon, which is inflated to expand the stent against the artery walls, providing support once the balloon is deflated and removed. Self-expanding stents, on the other hand, are made from materials that allow them to expand spontaneously when deployed, adapting to the vessel's shape without the need for balloon inflation. Both types are used to maintain vessel patency in various clinical settings, each offering distinct advantages depending on the application.</p></p>
