<p><p>Poultry feed acidifiers are substances added to poultry diets to enhance feed preservation, improve gut health, and promote nutrient absorption. They help lower the pH of the feed, creating an environment that inhibits harmful bacteria while supporting beneficial microorganisms. Common types of acidifiers include organic acids, such as citric, lactic, and propionic acids, which not only extend shelf life but also enhance overall poultry performance by reducing incidents of gastrointestinal diseases.</p><p>The Poultry Feed Acidifiers Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.6% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by rising consumer awareness about poultry health, increasing demand for high-quality protein sources, and growing adoption of sustainable farming practices. Additionally, stringent regulations on antibiotic use in poultry feed have shifted focus toward natural alternatives like acidifiers, further boosting the market. </p><p>Furthermore, innovations in product formulations and a rising number of commercial poultry farms are reshaping the competitive landscape. The trend toward organic and clean-label products is also influencing consumer preferences, driving poultry producers to incorporate acidifiers into their feed formulations for improved animal welfare and product quality.</p></p>
