<p><p>Malleable iron castings are produced using two primary molding techniques: vertical molding and horizontal molding. Vertical molding involves creating molds with a vertical orientation, allowing for efficient casting of complex shapes and reducing the footprint of the machinery. In contrast, horizontal molding utilizes a side-oriented layout, facilitating easier handling of larger molds and improving production rates. Both methods cater to different production needs, impacting factors such as casting quality, cycle time, and overall manufacturing efficiency in the malleable iron castings market.</p></p>
