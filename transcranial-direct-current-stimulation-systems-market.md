<p><p>The Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS) market primarily involves the application of a constant low electrical current to modulate neuronal activity, enhancing cognitive function and treating mood disorders. Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS) employs alternating currents to influence brain rhythms, offering potential benefits for neuropsychiatric conditions. Transcranial Pulsed Current Stimulation (tPCS) uses short electrical pulses for targeted stimulation, while Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS) introduces random noise currents to increase cortical excitability and facilitate learning processes, expanding therapeutic applications across neuroscience.</p></p>
