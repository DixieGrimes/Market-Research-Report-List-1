<p><p>The Serum Amyloid A Test Kit market includes two main types: Time-resolved Immunofluorescence (TRIF) and Chemiluminescence Immunoassay (CLIA). TRIF utilizes fluorescent labels and time-resolved detection to enhance sensitivity and specificity for measuring serum amyloid A levels, making it suitable for research and clinical diagnostics. CLIA, on the other hand, employs chemiluminescent reactions to quantify biomolecules, offering high throughput and rapid results. Both methods are critical for diagnosing inflammatory conditions and monitoring patients at risk for amyloidosis.</p></p>
