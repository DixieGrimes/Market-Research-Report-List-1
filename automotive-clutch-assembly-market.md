Friction clutches are the most common, using friction plates to engage and disengage power transmission between the engine and transmission. Electromagnetic clutches rely on magnetic fields for smooth engagement, offering precise control and reduced wear. Other types include hydraulic and centrifugal clutches, catering to specific vehicle needs. Each type serves distinct applications, balancing performance, durability, and efficiency in automotive systems.</p></p>
