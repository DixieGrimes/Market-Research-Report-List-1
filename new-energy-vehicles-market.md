<p><p>New Energy Vehicles (NEVs) include HEVs (Hybrid Electric Vehicles), PHEVs (Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles), and EVs (Electric Vehicles). HEVs combine internal combustion engines with electric motors, using regenerative braking to recharge batteries. PHEVs also blend combustion engines and electric power but can be charged externally, offering extended electric-only range. EVs are fully electric, relying solely on batteries and electric motors, producing zero emissions. Each type caters to different consumer needs, balancing fuel efficiency, environmental impact, and driving range.</p></p>
