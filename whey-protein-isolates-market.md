<p><p>The whey protein isolate market features three primary types based on the source: cow, buffalo, and goat. Cow whey protein isolates are the most common, rich in essential amino acids and widely used in supplements and food products. Buffalo whey protein, less prevalent, offers a creamier texture and higher protein content, appealing to niche markets. Goat whey protein isolates, though rare, are sought after for their digestibility and lower allergenic potential, catering to those with sensitivities to cow's milk.</p></p>
