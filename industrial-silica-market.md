<p><ul><li>Crystalline State</li><li>Amorphous State</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The industrial silica market is divided into two primary types based on its structure: crystalline and amorphous. Crystalline silica, found in forms like quartz, is widely used in glassmaking, ceramics, and construction due to its strength and durability. Amorphous silica, often derived from materials like diatomaceous earth, is utilized in applications such as filtration, adsorption, and as a filler in various products. Both types serve distinct roles in industries, contributing to the demand and growth of the silica market.</p></p>
